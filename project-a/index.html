Scientific Name:

NoctiFlor

Common Name:

Moonless Wing

Discovered by / at:

A criminal justice lawyer named Lily when she was finishing up paperwork at her lawfirm. It was a quarter past midnight when she decided to leave the office and stumbled across the Noctiflor. Rumorsvsay that nothing good ever happens after midnight

Family:

Botanica & Magicae Creaturae

Habitat:

Thrives underneath ancient transit systems, forgotten gardens, and most importantly, the abandoned tunnels of London King's Cross Station

Appearance:

Appears as a glowing botanical creature; some say the seed bears a resemblance to the golden snitch

Key Characteristics:

  • Distorts memories and time
  • Only transforms under a full moon
  • Has a cluster of petals similar to a Hydrangea
  • The scent reminds people of the air before a thunderstorm

What we know so far about the creature? The Noctiflor (Moonless Wing) lifecycle is tied to the lunar phases. The flower blooms during the full moon, but dies during the new moon. Some say that upon its death, a flurry of butterflies emerges. However, there is nothing good about the sighting of a Noctiflor. As a result, Noctiflor is an omen as beautiful as it is unsettling.

Guidelines for human interaction with the creature: The Noctiflor is best observed under a full moon. However, you must leave the area before the Noctiflor begins to die. Those present may be transported into a lost timeline or completely lose their memory.
